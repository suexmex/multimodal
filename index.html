Apart

Parents think about what is right for their families. They will do anything to give their children the best life they can give them. Even if it means leaving their families, leaving what is so familiar for something so foreign and unwelcoming. My parents took that chance. They left everything. First my father left when I was only two days born. I did not know who my father was, all I knew was that a man sent me dresses through the mail and I grew up believing that my grandfather was my father. Years passed, 4 years to be exact, before I met the man I would now call papa. But my coming to America left my brothers alone, without a mother or father, and with the memories that we left behind to keep them by.

Now we are together but with the same fear of being torn apart once more. With the political tension happening on the topic of immigration, many families are being affected, one being my own. My family would again be seen parting away. Due to my parents not having the legal statuses to stay in America, but making sure my sisters and I did, I would be forced to leave behind everything that I had now adapted to... friends, family, and the customs of American life. Everything is put on hold in these types of situations. I can never promise anything to my family because of the risk of having the life that I am now familiar with being taken away from me.

Secrets that soon turn to refreshed memories of the family they then had to leave behind for the idea of making their lives better.
